# Attendance

Free church class for kids and teens. Attendance will be inconsistent
— sports, family travel, illness, rough weeks at home. Policy reflects
that reality.

## Defaults

- **No formal attendance requirement.** Kids show up because they
  want to or because their parents bring them. Adding a stick creates
  problems you don't want.
- **Track attendance casually anyway.** A simple checklist per
  session, not for enforcement, but so you notice patterns. Three
  weeks missed in a row → check in with the student or their
  parents. Often it's nothing; occasionally it's something you can
  help with.
- **No special re-teaching for absentees.** They read the chapter,
  do the catch-up exercise (the **If you missed this session**
  section), ask their buddy when they come back. You don't re-run
  the lesson one-on-one. This sounds harsh; it's actually kinder —
  it respects the student's time and capability, and it preserves
  your prep time for the next session.
- **Two-strikes courtesy.** A student returning after 3+ consecutive
  absences gets ~5 min catch-up at the start of class — enough to
  participate today, not a full re-teach. Beyond that, they
  self-study.

## The exception: missed milestone sessions

Milestones are showable artifacts; they matter. If a kid misses the
session where the milestone project is introduced and worked on,
make sure they get a chance to do it — either in a later session's
Part B, or at home with buddy support. **Don't let a student skip
the "I built something" moment.** That moment is what carries
motivation through the dry parts.

## What this is downstream of

This policy follows from two things in `CURRICULUM-DECISIONS.md`:

- The **goal hierarchy** (logical thinking, practical tool, career
  foundation): a kid who attends 60% of sessions and engages with
  what they do attend is still well-served. The goals don't require
  perfect attendance.
- The **handouts-are-the-textbook** decision: every chapter is
  written so a student can catch up on their own. The "If you
  missed this session" section is a first-class part of every
  handout, not an afterthought. The textbook *is* the absentee
  recovery system.
